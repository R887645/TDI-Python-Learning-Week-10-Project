# Week 10: Advanced Seaborn Visualizations & Supply Chain Insights

In this Week 10 project, my learning advanced from Matplotlib fundamentals into statistical visualizations with Seaborn. The focus was on building smooth, professional plots that reveal deeper relationships in the supply chain data — moving from simple charts to multi-dimensional dashboards.

Where Week 9 emphasized clarity through basic chart types, Week 10 was about unlocking richer patterns with violin plots, box plots, KDE distributions, and correlation heatmaps, all tied together in a comprehensive dashboard.

![Intro](https://github.com/R887645/TDI-Python-Learning-Week-10-Project/blob/main/intro.png?raw=true)

---

## 🛠️ Key Skills Practiced

- Creating box plots, violin plots, strip/swarm plots, and KDE distributions  
- Overlaying plots (box + strip, histogram + KDE) to show both distributions and outliers  
- Building multi-panel dashboards with Seaborn subplots  
- Using consistent color palettes and styling for professional presentation  
- Saving charts as image files for reporting and communication  

---

## 📊 What I Implemented

### 1️⃣ Box + Strip Plots: Cost by Category & Reliability by Supplier

- Compared total cost distributions across product categories  
- Evaluated on-time delivery performance across suppliers  
- Revealed which categories are most expensive and which suppliers are most reliable

### 2️⃣ KDE Plot: Cost Distribution by Delivery Status

- Plotted smooth cost distributions for on-time vs late orders  
- Added histogram underneath for raw data visibility  
- Vertical line shows mean cost → easy comparison of cost patterns  


---

### 3️⃣ Multi-Panel Dashboard: Comprehensive Supply Chain View

Built a 2×2 Seaborn dashboard combining:

- Violin plot of cost by supplier  
- Box plot of delivery speed categories  
- Heatmap of numeric correlations  
- Count plot of product category order volumes

This project showed me how advanced visualization deepens analysis:

- Box/violin plots revealed supplier cost variability  
- KDE plots clarified distribution differences between on-time and late orders
